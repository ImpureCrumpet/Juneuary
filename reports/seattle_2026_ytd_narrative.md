### False springs — how many, and when

Seattle's taxonomy tracks three spring lies before the real thing. This year hit all of them:

1. **Fool's Spring (Feb)** — February spent most of its days in the 45–55°F range with occasional sun. Not shorts weather, but warm enough to feel like the corner was turned. Then mid-month collapsed back toward 40°F and delivered **1.8" snow on Feb 18** — the winteriest day of the whole season.
2. **Spring of Deception (Mar)** — March *looked* like spring in the blossoms-and-rain sense but ran **cool and soaking**. Highs averaged 51°F (3°F below normal). The deception wasn't warmth; it was **weather that felt like April while the calendar said March**.
3. **Third Winter (Apr)** — April warmed up (two separate 5-day stretches at 55°F+), then **Apr 15 snow** and a run of cold rain snapped the illusion. Flowering trees + hail/snow = peak Seattle.

**Actual Spring** finally showed up in late April and ran through most of May: a **27-day stretch from Apr 18–May 14** with only brief cool interruptions, including the year's first 80°F day (May 4).

**Caveats worth knowing:**

- Recent days (last ~7) blend forecast into the pipeline; re-fetch before quoting specific highs.
